Man, Woman, & Family

A weekend in O'Fallon, Missouri, given to the Bible's vision for manhood, womanhood, and the home.

September 25 – 26, 2026 Liberty Church, O'Fallon, Missouri Hosted by Covenant Family Church

Plate I — "A Conference Hosted by Covenant Family Church (OPC)"

Over two days this autumn, three pastors of the Orthodox Presbyterian Church will gather with the saints in O'Fallon to consider, from the Scriptures, what it means to be man, woman, and family in this generation. Friday evening through Saturday afternoon, with sessions for parents, young people, and children.

The Speakers

Three pastors on the family.

Faithful ministers from Missouri, Wisconsin, and Georgia.

No. I

Peter Bringe

Covenant Family OPC · Wentzville, Missouri↗

Peter Bringe has lived most of his life here in St. Charles County and is happy to call it home. He has a long history with Covenant Family Church, having first become a member with his family when he was twelve. Peter holds a BA in Bible and Theology from Whitefield College and an MDiv from Covenant Theological Seminary. After serving as a pastoral intern and deacon, he was called as pastor and ordained on October 9, 2018. Peter and his wife Melody have been blessed with seven children. On the side, he enjoys folk music, history, and the outdoors.
No. II

Brian De Jong

Grace OPC · Sheboygan, Wisconsin↗

Born in Bozeman, Montana in 1963, Pastor De Jong grew up across Michigan, Iowa, California, New Jersey, and Illinois. He earned a BA in Cinema and Photography from Southern Illinois University-Carbondale and an MDiv from Reformed Theological Seminary in Jackson. Ordained in 1993 (PCA) for campus ministry at the University of Florida, he later planted a church in Marion, Illinois, and pastored Covenant OPC in Brighton, Michigan. Since 2005 he has served as pastor of Grace OPC in Sheboygan, and as chairman of the Candidates and Credentials Committee and Historian of the Presbytery of Wisconsin and Minnesota. He and DeLou have six children and eight grandchildren.
No. III

Mike Myers

Heritage OPC · Royston, Georgia↗

After graduating from Greenville Presbyterian Theological Seminary in May 2013, Mike Myers was called to be the organizing pastor of Heritage Presbyterian Church (OPC). He was ordained on December 6, 2013. When the church particularized in September 2017, Heritage called Pastor Myers to stay on as the minister. Mike and his wife Katy have eight children.
