Speakers

Three Pastors of the OPC

Hear from three faithful ministers of the Orthodox Presbyterian Church as they unfold the Scriptures on manhood, womanhood, and the home.

Rev. Peter Bringe

Covenant Family OPC · Wentzville, MO

Rev. Peter Bringe has lived most of his life in St. Charles County and is happy to call it home. He has a long history with Covenant Family Church, having first become a member with his family at age twelve. He holds a BA in Bible and Theology from Whitefield College and an MDiv from Covenant Theological Seminary. After serving as a pastoral intern and deacon, he was called as pastor and ordained on October 9, 2018. Peter and his wife Melody have been blessed with seven children. On the side, he enjoys folk music, history, and the outdoors.

Rev. Brian De Jong

Grace OPC · Sheboygan, WI

Born in Bozeman, Montana in 1963, Pastor De Jong earned a BA in Cinema and Photography from Southern Illinois University-Carbondale before receiving his MDiv from Reformed Theological Seminary in Jackson. Ordained in 1993 in the PCA, he ministered to international students at the University of Florida, planted a church in Marion, Illinois, and served Covenant OPC in Brighton, Michigan. Since 2005 he has been pastor of Grace OPC in Sheboygan, where he also serves as chairman of the Candidates and Credentials Committee and as Archivist and Historian of the Presbytery of Wisconsin and Minnesota. He and DeLou have six children and eight grandchildren.

Rev. Mike Myers

Heritage OPC · Royston, GA

After graduating from Greenville Presbyterian Theological Seminary in May 2013, Mike Myers was called to be the organizing pastor of Heritage Presbyterian Church (OPC). He was ordained on December 6, 2013. When the church particularized in September 2017, Heritage called Pastor Myers to stay as the minister. Mike and his wife Katy have eight children.
